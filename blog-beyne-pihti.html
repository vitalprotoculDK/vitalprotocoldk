What Signals Does the Body Give Before a Brain Clot?

A stroke doesn't come suddenly — your body warns you hours before

⏱ ~5 min read✦ Deniz KAT
← Back to Blog
⚠️ Important: This content is for informational purposes only and does not replace medical diagnosis. In case of suspicious symptoms, seek medical attention immediately.

Every year, 15 million people worldwide have a stroke. In the vast majority of these cases, patients had dismissed previous symptoms as "fatigue" or "stress." Yet these symptoms were actually SOS signals.

Early Warning Signs

URGENT
Sudden, severe headache — A type never experienced before, like "a hammer hitting the head." Starts suddenly, without cause.
URGENT
Sudden vision problems in one or both eyes — Blurriness, double vision, or temporary blindness. It may pass in minutes — don't be misled.
URGENT
Difficulty speaking or understanding — Struggling to find words, speaking nonsensically, or difficulty understanding others.
CAUTION
Numbness/weakness in face, arm, or leg — Usually affects one side of the body.

The FAST Test

When you notice symptoms, call emergency services

F — Face: Ask them to smile. Is one side drooping?
A — Arms: Ask them to raise both arms. Does one drift down?
S — Speech: Ask them to repeat a simple sentence. Is it slurred or strange?
T — Time: If any of these are present, call emergency services immediately!
80% of strokes are preventable. Early detection makes treatment possible.

¿Qué Señales da el Cuerpo Antes de un Coágulo Cerebral?

Un ictus no llega de repente — tu cuerpo te avisa horas antes

⏱ ~5 min de lectura✦ Deniz KAT
← Volver al Blog
⚠️ Importante: Este contenido es solo informativo y no reemplaza el diagnóstico médico. Ante síntomas sospechosos, acuda a un centro de salud de inmediato.

Cada año, 15 millones de personas en todo el mundo sufren un ictus. En la gran mayoría de estos casos, los pacientes habían descartado síntomas previos como "cansancio" o "estrés." Sin embargo, estos síntomas eran en realidad señales de SOS.

Señales de Advertencia Tempranas

URGENTE
Dolor de cabeza repentino y severo — Un tipo nunca antes experimentado, como "un martillo golpeando la cabeza."
URGENTE
Problemas visuales repentinos — Visión borrosa, visión doble o ceguera temporal. Puede pasar en minutos, no se deje engañar.
URGENTE
Dificultad para hablar o entender — Dificultad para encontrar palabras, hablar sin sentido.
El 80% de los ictus son prevenibles. La detección temprana hace posible el tratamiento.
